Biography

Multi-national musician Sarah Marine is currently serving as principal flute of the Lebanese National Philharmonic Orchestra. Sarah previously spend ten years in London, where she studied her Master of Arts (Merit, 2021) at the Royal Academy of Music, having previously graduated with first class honors from Trinity Laban. Her awards include Third Prize in the Leopold Bellan International Competition (2023), the TCM Silver Medal for Wind Brass and Percussion (2020), the first prize in the Harold Clarke Competition (2018), the Berryl Maggs prize (2018), the runner - up prize of the TCM Soloist Competition ( 2019) and a Jonathan Myall Flute Prize (2019). Sarah has also performed with the Philharmonia Orchestra, the London Classical Orchestra, the Covent Garden Chamber Orchestra, the Horizon Orchestra and she plays regularly with the Ernest Read Symphony Orchestra. She is a dedicated chamber musician, and likes to rethink programmes, incorporating poetry, philosophy and song. Alongside performing, Sarah is a dedicated teacher. She has taught for various institutions including Junior Royal Academy of Music alongside her colleague and past teacher Anna Pope . Sarah is very grateful for support from the Countess of Munster Musical Trust, both during her studies at the Academy and in acquiring her beautiful gold Muramatsu flute. In her free time, Sarah has decided to learn how to code this website.
